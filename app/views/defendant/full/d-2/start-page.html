Respond to a money claim

You need to respond to the money claim within 14 days of receiving the claim.

Your response will be sent to the claimant (the person or organisation who says you owe them money). The claimant can apply for a County Court Judgment (CCJ) against you if you don't respond in time. They can then use this to get the money from you. For example, they could hire bailiffs.

A CCJ may make it harder to get credit, such as a mobile phone contract, credit card or mortgage.

If you think you’re owed money

If you believe the claimant owes you money you can make a claim against them (‘counterclaim’). You can do this as part of your response to the claim against you.

If you can't afford to pay the money back

You might be able to get free legal advice about dealing with debt from organisations like StepChange. Do this after you finish responding to the claim against you.
