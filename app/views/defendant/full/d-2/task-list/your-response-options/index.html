Your response options

Completing your claim

Admit all of the claim

If you admit all of the claim is fair you can contact the claimant yourself and pay the money. Ask them to let the court know when they have your payment.

If you can't afford to pay it all at once you can suggest a repayment plan for them to consider.

Admit part of the claim

If you think the claim is too high you can offer the amount you think you owe as part of your defence. You’ll get the chance to explain why your offer is the amount you believe you owe.

Reject the claim

If you don't accept you owe the amount of money claimed you can prepare and submit your defence online. You might have to go to a hearing - it can take up to 7 months to get a hearing date.

Settle out of court

You might be able to resolve the dispute without going to court. You can do this as part of completing your defence to the claim.

If you reject the claim or admit you owe less than the full amount claimed, you can make an offer or ask for mediation to settle the claim.

Make an offer

You can try to settle the claim by making the claimant an offer. For example, you could offer to repair damage you have caused to the claimant’s property or replace faulty goods you supplied them.

If the claimant rejects your offer you’ll have to go a hearing. The claimant can’t mention that you made them an offer at the hearing.

Mediate

You can ask for mediation to settle the claim. If the claimant agrees to use the mediation service, you could settle the claim without going to court.
