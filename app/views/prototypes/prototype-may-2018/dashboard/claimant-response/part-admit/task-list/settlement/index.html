How settlement agreements work

A settlement agreement is a legal agreement between you and the defendant describing how and when they'll repay you.

If the defendant signs your agreement, the claim is put on hold. You can't issue a CCJ against them unless they break the terms of the agreement.

If they don't think the repayment plan is affordable, they can ask a judge to make a new repayment plan.
