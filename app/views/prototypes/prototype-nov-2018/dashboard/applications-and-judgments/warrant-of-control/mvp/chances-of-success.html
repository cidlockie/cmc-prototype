Apply for a warrant of control

You should only apply for a warrant of control if you believe Mary Richards has either:

  • the amount you're trying to recover
  • items that could be taken by a bailiff and sold at auction to raise that amount
What bailiffs can and can't take

Bailiffs can take luxury items, for example a TV or games console.

They cannot take:

  • things Mary Richards needs to live, such as clothes, a cooker or a bed
  • pets or guide dogs
  • a phone or mobile phone
  • work tools and equipment worth a total of less than Â£1,350
  • medicine or medical equipment
  • anything bought on finance, such as a car bought using hire purchase
  • someone else's belongings, such as their partner's computer

When the warrant has been issued, the court will phone Mary Richards asking for payment.

If Mary Richards doesn't pay within 7 days, a bailiff will visit the address you gave within 15 days of the issue of the warrant. The bailiff usually visits up to 3 times.
